Journal Entry — January 5, 2024
Period: 2024-01

Started the year feeling restless. I keep thinking about whether I'm in the right career. Everyone around me seems so certain about what they want. I'm 28 and I still don't know. Sarah says I overthink everything, and maybe she's right. But how do you just *know*?

Read a book about people who changed careers in their 30s. It helped, a little. Maybe the answer isn't to find certainty but to get comfortable with uncertainty.

Resolution for this year: say yes to things that scare me. Let's see how long that lasts.

Journal Entry — February 14, 2024
Period: 2024-02

Valentine's Day alone again. Not sad about it, actually. Spent the evening cooking a complicated pasta dish from scratch. Burned the garlic. Ate it anyway.

Had a weird moment of clarity today: I think I've been so focused on finding "the one" that I forgot to enjoy being just me. Being alone isn't the same as being lonely. I had a good day. That counts for something.

Journal Entry — March 22, 2024
Period: 2024-03

Job interview tomorrow. Not sure I even want it, but I applied on a whim three weeks ago and now here we are. It's in a different city. Portland. I've never even been to Portland.

Called mom to talk about it. She said "the best decisions are the ones that scare you the most." Classic mom wisdom. I think she's right.

I'm going to pack a bag tonight and decide in the morning. If I get the offer, I might actually take it.

Journal Entry — May 10, 2024
Period: 2024-05

I moved to Portland. It's real now. The apartment is tiny, my car is full of boxes, and I know exactly one person here (Sarah's cousin). But the city is beautiful — all these green spaces and coffee shops and bookstores.

Started the new job this week. It's overwhelming. Everyone speaks in acronyms I don't understand. But there's something electric about starting over. Like I'm a new character in a video game and I get to choose my stats.

I miss my old friends. But I don't miss my old life.

---

Journal Entry — July 4, 2024
Period: 2024-07

Three months in Portland. Made a friend! Her name is Maya, and she's in my D&D group (yes, I joined a D&D group. 2024 me is wild). She's a graphic designer and we bonded over hating the same coffee shop.

Work is... work. Some days I love it, some days I wonder what I was thinking. But the view from my apartment window at sunset makes up for a lot.

I think I'm becoming someone new. Not sure who yet, but I like the process of finding out.

Journal Entry — September 15, 2024
Period: 2024-09

Had dinner with Sarah (she visited!). She said I seem different — more relaxed, more myself. I think she's right. There's something about starting fresh that forces you to shed the versions of yourself that don't fit anymore.

We talked about old times and it felt like visiting a museum of someone else's life. Is that growth? Feeling disconnected from who you used to be?

Maya came too and they got along great. Two worlds colliding. It was nice.

I'm starting to feel like Portland is home.

Journal Entry — November 30, 2024
Period: 2024-11

Thanksgiving alone (by choice). Cooked a full turkey dinner for one and it was glorious. Called my family on video chat. My nephew said "you live in a computer now" because he saw me on the screen.

Got promoted at work. Six months in and they're giving me more responsibility. I must be doing something right.

Looking back at my January entry, I barely recognize that person. She was so afraid. I'm still afraid sometimes, but now I know that's not a reason to stay put.

The best decisions are the ones that scare you most. Mom was right.

Journal Entry — December 31, 2024
Period: 2024-12

End of year reflection.

This year I:

- Made new friends
- Got promoted

- Started saying yes to things that scare me
